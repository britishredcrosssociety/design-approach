Design Approach

British Red Cross' design phases and examples of how we do them in practice

  1. Discover
  2. Test different options
  3. Start small and scale
  4. Improve continuously

1. Discover

Speak to the people you are designing for, to understand their wants and needs. Gain a detailed understanding of the problem you need to solve.

  • Wrapping up discovery by Vicky Houghton-Price
  • Mapping unmet needs by Harry Trimble



2. Test different ideas

Develop and test ideas with people you are designing for to find which best solves the problem. Iterate ideas that show promise and discard ones that don’t.

  • Testing a rota prototype with Emergency Response volunteers by Jo Straw
  • Testing a rota prototype with Emergency Response volunteers by Laura Basegmezer



3. Start small and scale

Test one promising solution in the ‘real world’. Start small and learn quickly to reduce risk. Iterate or pivot based on the insights you gain.

  • Making a manual for support line volunteers by Harry Trimble



4. Improve continuously

Now proved, embed the solution in the dedicated business area. Continuously improve based on people's wants, needs and behaviours.

  • It’s time to Level Up by Adrien Barbusse
